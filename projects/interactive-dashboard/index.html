Generative AI Business Process Automation

Built controlled generative AI workflows using Claude and OpenAI APIs to reduce manual reporting effort while keeping outputs reviewable, structured, and fit for business use.

Year: 2026
Outcome: 70% reporting time reduction
Focus: Claude API • OpenAI API • Agentic Workflows

Project Overview

This project focused on business process automation opportunities where generative AI could remove repetitive reporting effort without losing human oversight. I used Claude and OpenAI APIs to design and implement workflows that turned manual analyst tasks into structured, repeatable AI-assisted processes with clear review checkpoints.

Objectives

  • Reduce manual reporting effort by 70% through AI-assisted automation
  • Identify high-value intervention points across reporting and operational workflows
  • Define requirements, controls, and success measures for each AI use case
  • Design reliable outputs that remain reviewable by business stakeholders
  • Create reusable workflow patterns for future automation opportunities

Solution Design

  • Use Case Mapping: Identified repetitive tasks, input dependencies, and approval checkpoints
  • Prompt Workflow Design: Structured prompts and output formats for repeatable business use
  • Human-in-the-Loop Review: Preserved validation points for sensitive or high-impact outputs
  • Output Standardisation: Created predictable summaries, drafts, and report-ready responses
  • Scalability Planning: Designed the workflows so they could expand into broader agentic automation later

Technical Implementation

  • Models: Claude Sonnet/Opus and OpenAI API workflows
  • Workflow Logic: Multi-step prompts, structured outputs, and task-specific routing
  • Inputs: Business requirements, operational notes, reporting templates, and source data extracts
  • Controls: Output review checkpoints, scope constraints, and use-case-specific validation
  • Delivery Focus: Practical automation designed for real business use and controlled adoption

Business Analysis Contribution

  • Requirements Definition: Captured workflow needs, output expectations, and exception cases
  • Process Mapping: Assessed where AI could add value without introducing avoidable risk
  • Success Measures: Defined time savings, consistency, and usability metrics for each use case
  • Stakeholder Alignment: Balanced technical possibilities with business practicality and adoption readiness
  • Responsible Use: Focused on controlled AI assistance rather than uncontrolled full automation

Business Impact

  • 70% time reduction: Manual reporting and drafting effort was significantly reduced
  • Higher consistency: Reusable prompt flows improved output structure and repeatability
  • Faster delivery: Teams could move from raw inputs to usable summaries much more quickly
  • Better prioritisation: Analysts could spend more time on interpretation and decision support
  • Foundation for agentic AI: The work created a base for more advanced workflow orchestration

Next Steps

  • Extend the workflows with retrieval and knowledge-grounding patterns
  • Add deeper evaluation for output quality and failure cases
  • Expand to more complex agentic use cases with stronger orchestration
  • Formalise the approach into reusable internal automation playbooks
