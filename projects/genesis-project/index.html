Genesis Energy: Supply Chain Analysis and Recommendations

Supply chain design assignment analysing Genesis Energy's end-to-end energy workflow, key operational challenges, and recommendations for resilience, grid modernization, and renewable transition.

Type: Supply chain analysis report
Course: BSNS8051 • Semester 1, 2025
Focus: Energy supply chain • Sustainability • Demand planning
Open Full Report (PDF)

Project Overview

This report examined Genesis Energy as a major New Zealand energy provider operating across electricity generation, transmission, distribution support, and retail services. The analysis focused on how Genesis balances renewable expansion, thermal backup generation, fuel procurement risk, and long-term sustainability commitments inside a complex national energy supply chain.

What This Demonstrates

The project demonstrates structured supply chain analysis, sector research, strategic recommendation writing, and the ability to translate operational complexity into clear improvement priorities.

Why Genesis Energy

Genesis was selected because it holds a significant position in New Zealand's energy market and operates a diverse generation mix that includes thermal, hydro, wind, and solar assets. That mix makes it a strong case for analysing resilience, renewable integration, and operational tradeoffs during energy transition.

Supply Chain Workflow Reviewed

  • Fuel procurement: Coal, gas, and biomass sourcing for thermal generation at Huntly Power Station
  • Electricity generation: Thermal, hydro, wind, and solar generation assets across New Zealand
  • Transmission and distribution: Coordination with Transpower and regional lines companies
  • Retail operations: Delivery of electricity, gas, LPG, and digital energy services to more than 500,000 customers

Key Challenges Identified

  • Gas shortages increasing dependence on coal reserves at Huntly Power Station
  • Procurement exposure to Indonesian coal export restrictions and logistics disruption
  • Volatility in renewable generation caused by low rainfall, low wind, and solar variability
  • Tension between short-term energy reliability and long-term decarbonisation goals under the Gen35 strategy

Analysis and Strategic View

The report argued that Genesis needs a balanced transition path: maintaining reliable backup generation in the short term while accelerating investment in lower-carbon alternatives such as biomass, battery storage, solar, and stronger demand management systems. It also highlighted the value of digital transformation, including AI-enabled demand prediction and capacity planning, to improve both operational resilience and customer service.

Recommendations

  • Establish multi-year biomass supplier contracts for more stable transition fuel supply
  • Increase investment in grid modernization to support renewable integration efficiently
  • Apply demand forecasting models to optimize production and energy distribution planning
  • Build stronger partnerships with research organisations to develop innovative energy solutions
  • Expand digital transformation initiatives to improve resilience and customer demand prediction
